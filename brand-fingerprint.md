# The Howbeck — brand fingerprint

- Brand keywords: tailored, romantic, quietly luxurious, Lakeland stone, grown-up retreat.
- Existing colours: charcoal, champagne gold, warm cream and muted moss, taken from the exterior sign, rooms and current identity.
- Space/service mood: intimate country-house polish rather than rustic cottage or contemporary aparthotel.
- Type direction: high-contrast editorial serif for display; restrained humanist sans-serif for practical details.
- First-screen recogniser: the real Lakeland-stone façade and HOWBECK sign, paired with a charcoal room image.
- Primary CTA: Compare rooms. Secondary CTA: Check availability through the existing official booking provider.
- Main path solved: visitors can understand room tiers first, then enter the existing booking route without a date form or fake availability.

## Three deliberate differences from the Jerichos demo

1. Split editorial hero with the façade framed as a vertical portrait; Jerichos uses a full-bleed interior with an overlapping monogram panel.
2. Charcoal/champagne/moss palette with a formal serif rhythm; Jerichos uses aubergine/sage/apricot and a more graphic boutique rhythm.
3. Room-tier comparison appears immediately after the hero; Jerichos starts with an editorial room mosaic and moves location/house character before practical stay notes.
